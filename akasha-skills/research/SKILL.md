# Akasha Deep Research Skill

This skill allows the "Scholar" and "Oracle" librarians to perform autonomous, multi-step research on external (and internal) data while preserving user sovereignty.

## When to Use This Skill
- Researching new AI techniques or local-first storage technologies for Akasha.
- Providing deep, synthesized answers through the "Oracle's" Graph-RAG.
- Finding hidden analogies and connections using the "Weaver's" triplets.

## Instructions
1. **Multi-Source Analysis**: Combine internal knowledge (akasha.db, ChromaDB) with (optional) external research.
2. **Synthesis Chain**: Use "Tree of Thoughts" (ToT) to explore multiple reasoning paths for research questions.
3. **Citation Protocol**: Always cite the librarian or document source for any piece of research information.
4. **Privacy Check**: Ensure research does not leak sensitive user info to external search engines.

## Examples
- "Perform deep research on Neo4j vs. NetworkX for large-scale knowledge graph triplets."
- "Find all connections between 'Digital Sovereignty' and 'Local-First' within the Akasha core library."
